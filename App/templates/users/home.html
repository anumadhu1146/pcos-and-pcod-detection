What is PCOS?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that affects people with ovaries, often during their reproductive years. It is characterized by irregular menstrual cycles, elevated levels of male hormones (androgens), and the presence of cysts in the ovaries.

  • Common symptoms include irregular periods, acne, weight gain, and excessive hair growth.
  • PCOS can also lead to complications such as infertility, diabetes, and cardiovascular issues.
  • Management often includes lifestyle changes, medications to regulate menstrual cycles and hormones, and sometimes fertility treatments.

PCOS can be diagnosed through a combination of physical exams, blood tests to measure hormone levels, and ultrasound imaging to check for ovarian cysts. Early diagnosis and management are important to address symptoms and prevent long-term health issues. If you experience symptoms or have concerns, consult a healthcare provider for appropriate evaluation and care.

Understanding PCOS

Introduction to PCOS

PCOS is a common hormonal disorder that affects the ovaries, leading to irregular periods and elevated androgen levels. It can impact various aspects of health, including reproductive and metabolic functions.

Symptoms of PCOS

Symptoms of PCOS may include irregular menstrual cycles, acne, weight gain, excessive hair growth, and thinning hair. These symptoms can vary widely among individuals.

Causes and Risk Factors

The exact cause of PCOS is unknown, but factors such as genetics, insulin resistance, and inflammation may contribute. Risk factors include a family history of PCOS and obesity.

Diagnosis and Treatment

PCOS is diagnosed through a combination of medical history, physical examination, blood tests, and ultrasound. Treatment may include lifestyle changes, hormonal medications, and, if needed, fertility treatments.
